Only being a short distance from the city, Wadi Ghalilah naturally is a giant tourist attraction for many reasons. Look at it, the mountain range and weather are both exceedingly beautiful in this picture. These kind of conditions are common place at Wadi Ghalilah.

Hatta is a gorgeous 115 km (71 miles) drive east of Dubai City, with a gentler climate due to its location in the steep Hajjar Mountains. Both the locals And travelers love to visit Hatta as it is a great way to escape the heat. Its true Arabic "historical town," which includes 30 loam dwellings, an old Juma mosque from the 18th century, and two military towers, is worth visiting. Adrenaline seekers frequently travel to Hatta for off-road adventures in the desert or mountains, stopping at the Hatta Rock Pools, which are small, winding canyons of smooth rock with pools of blue-green water. Despite the town's lack of attractions and commerce.

Dubai is in an arid desert climate. This means that Dubai only ever has 2 seasons, winter and summer. As well as this, there are also 2 transitional periods, not significant enough to differentiate them from the main seasons of summer and winter.
